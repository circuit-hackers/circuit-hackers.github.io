Robotics brings many diciplines into application. We work to master them all.

Coding

Computer Science foundations: Programming skills bring robots to life! They also strengthen logic and reasoning skills - skills that are usable in the workforce.

Electronics

From transistors and capacitors, to microcontrollers, to ultrasonic sensors and humidity sensors, we put electronics to work!

Engineering

We design, craft and assemble our robot's machinery. Gears, levers, servos, CAD programs, 3D printers and materials of all sorts are our tools.

Creativity and Perseverance

Mind-energies are put to work in brainstorming and problem solving. We work together to succeed through a good dose of instruction and large amount of trial and error.

Math and Science

Math and science skills grow as they are put into practice by bringing ideas into the reality of a robot sensing and interacting with the world.

Friendship and Community

Working together we become friends, and seek new ways to serve our community. Our club members partner with and mentor one another.

Some of Our Projects

Here are some samples of recent projects our club members have worked on.

Volts, Blink'n Lights, and More

We learned the fundamental concepts of electricity, the basics of building a simple circuit, and controlling it with a programmed microcontroller.

Coding basics: Loops and Conditionals

How do you make a program flow? How do you repeat actions/behaviors and make decisions about new ones?

Sensor Explorations

Electronic sensors give robots the potential to "perceive" the world. We explored sensors for sound, light, heat, humidity, temperature, motion, and others.

Obstical Avoidance Robot

Using the knowledge gained in earlier projects, we built an wheeled-robot that can roam a room, avoiding bumping into furniture and walls.
